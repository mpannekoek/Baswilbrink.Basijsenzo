# User Stories

## Story Approach
- **Structure**: Hybrid
- **Primary Organization**: Feature-based stories aligned to landing-page sections
- **Validation Lens**: Journey-aware acceptance criteria that protect the end-to-end visitor experience
- **Placeholder Policy**: Placeholder content is acceptable for version one, but the implementation must make future replacement straightforward
- **Content Editability Policy**: Visitor-facing copy should be maintained through a centralized content resource instead of component-embedded strings
- **Mobile Priority**: Mobile usability is a first-class acceptance concern across all stories

## INVEST Notes
- **Independent**: Each story maps to a discrete page outcome or section responsibility
- **Negotiable**: Copy, imagery, and exact presentation details can evolve without breaking the story goal
- **Valuable**: Every story ties to a concrete visitor need or business objective
- **Estimable**: Scope is small enough for direct implementation on a single landing page
- **Small**: Stories are section-sized, not project-sized
- **Testable**: Each story includes observable acceptance criteria

## US-1: Welcoming First Impression
**As a** first-time or returning visitor  
**I want** the page to make a strong, warm, and local first impression immediately  
**So that** I feel Bas IJs & Zo is inviting, trustworthy, and worth visiting

**Primary Personas**: Dorpsbezoeker Daan, Gezinsplanner Gina, Bezoeker Ben

**Acceptance Criteria**
- The hero section presents the Bas IJs & Zo brand with a polished, distinctive visual identity.
- The visual direction feels playful and family-friendly without looking generic or childish.
- Black and orange are visibly dominant brand anchors.
- The page communicates a village-oriented local identity within the first screenful.
- The hero includes at least one clear practical action, such as visit, call, or route/contact.
- Hero, navigation, and CTA copy are structured so they can be updated without editing section component markup.
- The first impression remains strong and readable on mobile screens.

## US-2: Fast Practical Information
**As a** local or passing visitor  
**I want** to quickly find opening hours, contact details, and location cues  
**So that** I can decide whether to visit without searching through the whole page

**Primary Personas**: Dorpsbezoeker Daan, Bezoeker Ben

**Acceptance Criteria**
- Opening hours are visually easy to scan.
- Contact details are prominent and actionable.
- Address or route-related information is available without deep scrolling.
- The layout gives practical information enough emphasis relative to decorative content.
- The practical-information section is comfortable to use on mobile without precision tapping or zooming.
- Placeholder business details are presented in a centralized structured resource that is easy to replace later.

## US-3: Local Story And Personality
**As a** family-oriented or community-minded visitor  
**I want** to understand the history and local character of the parlor  
**So that** the business feels personal, rooted, and memorable

**Primary Personas**: Gezinsplanner Gina

**Acceptance Criteria**
- The page includes a dedicated history or about section.
- The copy and presentation reinforce a warm and trustworthy local identity.
- The section adds emotional context without obscuring practical information elsewhere on the page.
- The visual treatment fits the broader brand direction and remains readable on small screens.

## US-4: Taste Of The Week Spotlight
**As a** visitor deciding whether to stop by  
**I want** to see a featured taste or product highlight  
**So that** the page feels lively and gives me a fun reason to visit now

**Primary Personas**: Dorpsbezoeker Daan, Gezinsplanner Gina

**Acceptance Criteria**
- The page includes a clearly identifiable "taste of the week" or equivalent featured product area.
- The product spotlight feels playful and appetizing rather than purely informational.
- The section is small enough to support a landing page, but prominent enough to add personality.
- The content structure makes future flavor updates and related support copy straightforward without TSX edits.
- The spotlight works well in the mobile layout without becoming visually cramped.

## US-5: Natural Social Proof
**As a** cautious or new visitor  
**I want** to see believable positive feedback from other customers  
**So that** I can trust that the parlor is well regarded

**Primary Personas**: Gezinsplanner Gina, Bezoeker Ben

**Acceptance Criteria**
- The page includes a reviews or social-proof section.
- Review presentation feels natural and trustworthy rather than overly promotional.
- Placeholder reviews are clearly realistic in tone and are centrally structured so real approved reviews can replace them later without component rewrites.
- The review section supports the page's welcoming tone and does not overpower practical information.
- The review layout remains legible and balanced on mobile.

## US-6: Comfortable Mobile Experience
**As a** mobile visitor  
**I want** the landing page to be easy to read and use on my phone  
**So that** I can access key information and actions while on the move

**Primary Personas**: Dorpsbezoeker Daan, Gezinsplanner Gina, Bezoeker Ben

**Acceptance Criteria**
- The landing page is responsive across small and large screens.
- Core actions and key practical information remain easy to access on mobile.
- Primary navigation collapses into a clear hamburger menu on smaller screens and expands without layout breakage when activated.
- Section spacing, typography, and touch targets remain comfortable on smaller devices.
- Expressive visual styling does not compromise performance of the layout or information hierarchy on mobile.
- Mobile usability is treated as a core success condition, not an optional refinement.
